BILLIARD TABLE

The table used for pool is a six pocket table, or we can say six hole table. The pockets are four in four corners and two pockets are in the middle of the two lengths of the table. The table is rectangular in shape having a length of 2.75 metres and breadth of 1.37 metres.

CUE STICK

Cue is a stick used to hit the ball. The length of the cue stick is around 55 inches. There is no fixed length as some cue sticks are around 58 inches. The taper down the tip is 0.5 inch. However, in case of snooker, the tip of the cue is normally smaller in comparison.

BALL

The cue sports mainly depend on balls. There are normally three types of cue sports which are pool, snooker, and billiards. In each sport, different number of balls are used.

BRIDGE

Bridge is also called a rake and is used sometimes as a supporting instrument for the cue stick. At some point of time during the match, the ball may be far away from the player to take a shot. At this time the player uses bridge as a support which makes it easier for him to play a shot.

CHALK

Like the bridge, chalk is also not important equipment. It is optional for a player to use it. Chalk is rubbed on the tip of the cue stick. Normally, before every shot, a player rubs the tip with chalk to increase the friction between the tip and the ball in order to increase the hitting efficiency.

RACK

A rack is normally triangle in shape. It is used both in snooker and pool. If we are having an eight-ball pool, we can use a triangle rack and if we play a nine-ball pool, then the shape of rack will be of diamond shape. When the game starts, the rack is removed from the table.

About Me

Billiards is a cue sport played on a rectangular table with a set of balls and a cue stick. The game involves players using the cue stick to strike the cue ball, which in turn hits other balls on the table. The goal and rules vary depending on the specific type of billiards game being played. In traditional English billiards, players attempt to score points by potting balls or executing specific shots like cannons (hitting two balls with the cue ball). In other variations, such as pool and snooker, players aim to pot balls into pockets, following a sequence of rules that define the game's objective. Billiards requires skill, precision, and strategy, as players must plan their shots, control the cue ball’s movement, and anticipate their opponent’s moves.
